From the Inbound tab you can simulate a call, select your intent (book an appointment, ask about insurance, report an urgent need), and watch the AI carry the conversation naturally. The Outbound tab shows a CRM contact list (GoHighLevel-style) and generates dynamic call scripts per patient (name, provider, last visit, upcoming appointment). The Analytics tab shows 155 calls, 67% booking rate, 8% escalation rate, intent breakdown charts, and a full transcript log. The Automation flow lights up in real time as calls move through the pipeline: greeting to intent to booking to webhook to SMS. Click the escalation path and the transfer logic; it is the part clients always ask about.

**How I would build the real thing.** For production I would wire Vapi or Retell for telephony (both have robust inbound call + webhook APIs and GoHighLevel native integrations), Twilio for outbound dialing and SMS, and an n8n workflow as the orchestration layer: greeting intent classifier, slot-fill booking agent, CRM record lookup, webhook trigger, and escalation routing. The in-browser prototype shows the conversation design and UI, which is where the real product work lives. The infrastructure integration is straightforward once the conversation architecture is settled. I would instrument every call with a confidence score, log transcripts, and build the analytics layer on top of those logs. HIPAA-eligible call recording is a configuration flag in Vapi/Retell, not a custom build.
