The Problem

  • Flat 2D preview fails to build purchase confidence
  • Customers struggle to visualize how a graphic wraps around back and sides
  • Dead-click errors from poorly structured state management kill conversions
  • Script-blocking canvases slow homepage load, increasing bounce rate

What I Built

  • Photorealistic Three.js shirt with studio lighting and procedural knit normals
  • Full all-around texture wrap (2048px) from a single image upload
  • Separate front and back image slots with pixel-perfect UV alignment
  • Zustand state management, zero undefined is not an object crashes
  • Lazy-loaded canvas — homepage stays fast, customizer loads on demand

Core Features

🎽

Owayo-Level 3D Realism

Procedural knit normal map, studio three-point lighting, real-time orbit controls. Rivals owayo.com's configurator in perceived fidelity.

🖼️

Image Upload + Full Wrap

Upload any photo or graphic. It tiles seamlessly across the full 360 shirt (front px 512-1536, back/sides in the outer UV bands). Separate front and back slots supported.

🧠

Smart Alignment

One click centers the subject on the shirt front. WebGPU Tier-2 refinement when available; instant luminance-bbox fallback otherwise. No manual cropping required.

🎚️

Zoom, Crop and Position

Sliders for scale, X/Y offset, and rotation update the 3D render live. What the customer sees in the controls is exactly what prints.

🎨

Color Picker + Patterns

Six canvas-generated retro patterns plus a freeform color picker for panels. Patterns and image uploads can be layered or used independently.

🛒

Product Page + Cart

Size selector, localStorage cart persistence, customization thumbnail preview baked into the cart item. Full shop flow, not just a viewer.
